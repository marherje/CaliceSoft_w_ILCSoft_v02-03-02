Dead and Noisy Channels Analysis for CALICE Test Beam

Introduction

Determining dead and noisy channels is based on the analysis of the channel ADC spectra recorded during a PMNoiseRun. These spectra are provided by ahcBinHst from the calice_daq package.

Typical ADC spectrum of a channel in a noise run.

Only the mean of the distribution (the pedestal in case of a noise run) and the width (RMS) are used.

Definition of dead and noisy

Currently dead and noisy are only defined from the RMS.

Noisy

Looking at RMS sectrum of all channels one sees that most channels have an RMS around 40, but some channels (the noisy ones) have much larger RMS, causing a tail in the distribution.

The RMS spectrum of all channels.

Definiton of noisy: RMS > 140

Dead

A comparisan with data and LEDVCalib runs has shown that the peak at the lover end of the distribution contains basically only dead channels (see section "Comparing two runs").

The RMS spectrum of all channels.

Definiton of dead: RMS < 20.5

Software overview

The software basically consists of two parts

  • The root library libDeadAndNoisyTools which conveniently allows to access the RMS and pedestal values of one or several runs
  • The createBadChannelsList executable which creates the list of bad channels to be written to the database.
